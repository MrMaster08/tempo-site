Questions

The honest answers

Is Tempo really free?
Yes. The Mac app is a free download, and the free tier isn't a demo: unlimited tasks, lists and tags, natural-language quick add, Apple Calendar, habits with streaks and heatmaps, the full Grove focus system, widgets and the menu-bar command center. Plus is optional — you only need it for the connected features.
What does Tempo Plus add?
Cloud sync across your devices, sharing with the person you plan with (a shared calendar and shared lists), Google Calendar and Notion integrations, task templates and saved filters, and premium Grove scenes. It's US$1.99 a month, and every new account gets a 30-day free trial of the whole thing.
Why the right-click-to-open step on first launch?
This direct download is signed with a developer certificate rather than distributed through the App Store, so macOS shows a one-time notice. Right-click Tempo, choose Open, then Open again — after that it launches normally, forever.
When is the iPhone app coming?
It's built — tasks, calendar, habits, the Grove and widgets are all running on iPhone today — and it's coming to the App Store. With Plus sync turned on, your Mac and iPhone stay perfectly in step from day one.
Where does my data live?
On your device. Tempo works fully offline, and nothing leaves your Mac unless you turn on cloud sync or sharing — both optional, both under your control. The details are in our privacy policy.
Download

Get Tempo for Mac

Free, direct, about a minute from click to first task.

Download Tempo.dmg macOS 15 or later
  1. Open the download and slide Tempo into Applications — the installer window shows you the way. That's the whole install.
  2. First launch only: right-click Tempo → Open → Open. macOS shows a one-time notice because this build is developer-signed rather than App Store-distributed. It opens normally ever after.
  3. That's it. Create a sync account in Settings → Sync whenever you want your data on more than one device.
