Privacy policy

Last updated 2 July 2026

Tempo is built to keep your plans yours. This page describes everything the app does with data — it's short because there isn't much to tell.

Your data lives on your device

Tasks, lists, habits, focus sessions and settings are stored locally on your Mac or iPhone. Tempo has no advertising, no tracking code, and never sells data. The only thing the app reports home is an anonymous install counter — a random device identifier with the app version and device model, so we know how many devices run Tempo. It never includes your name, email, tasks, or anything you write. Your content never leaves your device unless you turn on sync.

If you create a sync account

Cloud sync is optional. When you create an account, the following is stored on the sync server so your devices (and, if you choose, the person you share with) can stay in sync:

  • Your email address and display name (to identify your account).
  • The content you sync: tasks, lists, habits, comments and related changes.
  • A securely hashed password — never the password itself.

Sharing only ever includes items you explicitly mark as shared. You can disconnect the person you share with, or delete your account and its server data, at any time from Settings → Sync.

Calendars

Apple Calendar events are read directly on your device and never leave it. Optional Google Calendar and work-calendar connections only read the calendars you choose to display.

Purchases

Tempo Plus subscriptions are processed entirely by Apple. Tempo never sees your payment details.

Notifications

Reminders and focus-session alerts are scheduled locally on your device.

Deleting your data

Settings → Data → Delete All Data erases everything stored by the app on that device, including preferences. Deleting a sync account removes your data from the server.

Questions? Reach out via the Tempo support page.
